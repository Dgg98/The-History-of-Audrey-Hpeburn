Legacy

Audrey Hepburn was a tireless advocate for children rights and became a UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador in 1988. She went on a mission to Ethiopia, where years of drought and civil strife had caused terrible famine.

She made a series of UNICEF field trips like visiting a polio vaccine project in Turkey, training programmes for women in Venezuela, initiatives for children living and working on the street in Ecuador and radio literacy programmes in El Salvador.

In 1992 she received the United States highest civilian award, the Presidential Medal of Freedom. Audrey devoted the final years of her life to UNICEF.
